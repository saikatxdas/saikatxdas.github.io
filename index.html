Saikat Das | Postdoc @ University of Florida

Hello and welcome — I’m Saikat Das

Exploring the extreme universe

I am a postdoctoral researcher in astrophysics and plasma physics at the University of Florida, USA, since September 2024. Before moving to USA, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Yukawa Institute for Theoretical Physics, Kyoto University, Japan (2021 - 2024) and a PhD student at Raman Research Institute, India (2016 - 2021). I study high-energy and particle astrophysics, focusing on cosmic rays, gamma rays, neutrinos, and dark matter. My work combines large-scale numerical simulations and theoretical modeling to explain observed data and to understand how cosmic rays are accelerated in compact objects and luminous gamma-ray sources. My research aims to predict multi-messenger signals for next-generation telescopes. I use particle-in-cell (PIC) simulations of relativistic magnetized turbulence to study particle acceleration in astrophysical plasmas. Currently, I am focusing on wave–particle interactions in electron-ion plasma and plasma instabilities to better understand energy transfer in these systems.
