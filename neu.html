Neutrinos and \(\gamma\)-ray blazars

For biological reasons, our perception of the Universe is based on the observation of photons, most trivially by staring at the night sky with bare eyes. The advancement of observational astronomy has allowed us to probe the Universe deeper with \(10^{12}\) cm radio waves to \(10^{-5}\) cm gamma-rays, covering an astonishing 17 orders of magnitude in wavelength. High-energy gamma-rays reveal the illuminated with Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) and galaxies which are radio-loud. But the Universe becomes impenetrable to electromagnetic radiation beyond TeV energies, due to \(e^+e^-\) pair production with background photons.

High-energy gamma rays can be produced by either leptonic or hadronic processes. NASA's Fermi-LAT telescope has observed a diffuse gamma-ray background up to hundreds of GeV energies. AGNs are the most abundant gamma-ray sources.
